The traditional agency model is broken. The traditional hiring model is too slow.

In today's hyper-competitive landscape, speed is the only currency that matters. Yet, companies spend months recruiting, vetting, and onboarding talent, only to find that the problem they needed to solve has evolved—or worse, the hire wasn't the right fit.

## The "Hiring Trap"

When you have a bottleneck—say, a legacy codebase that slows down feature release, or a conversion rate that's stuck at 1%—your instinct is to "hire a developer" or "hire a designer."

But hiring is a **capacity** solution, not an **outcome** solution.

When you hire, you are buying:
- 40 hours of presence per week
- The overhead of management
- The risk of bad fit
- The cost of benefits and equipment

You are *not* buying a solution to your problem. You are buying the *potential* for a solution, wrapped in a lot of administrative friction.
